# CULO Partnership Operating System — Ethics Charter

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Governing Document — Non-Negotiable  
**Scope:** Every commercial relationship inside CULO  
**References:** `docs/core/04-permission-blueprint.md`, `docs/engines/partner-engine/01-partner-engine-blueprint.md`

---

## Mission

Revenue must never come before trust.

Recommendations should strengthen relationships. Never weaken them.

The Partnership Operating System exists to create genuine opportunities — not advertisements disguised as recommendations.

The long-term success of CULO depends on protecting authentic human recommendations. The moment CULO becomes a platform where publishers write stories to earn affiliate commissions, it stops being a publishing platform and becomes an advertising network. That is not the mission.

The mission is to make it easier for publishers to share what they genuinely know — and to reward them fairly when that knowledge creates value for others.

---

## Philosophy

Trust is the product.

Knowledge creates trust. Stories create trust. Recommendations preserve trust. Revenue follows trust.

A publisher who recommends a product they use every day builds something that advertising cannot buy: a reader who believes them. That belief is the foundation of every business relationship in CULO.

This Ethics Charter is the governing document for every decision made inside the Partnership Operating System. When commercial pressure conflicts with trust, trust wins. Always.

---

## Core Ethical Principles

### 1. Authenticity

Every recommendation inside CULO must be based on genuine experience, genuine knowledge, or genuine belief.

A publisher who has never used a product may not create a recommendation for it. A publisher who recommends a product they have used but would not personally endorse may not do so for financial incentive.

CULO will never push publishers toward recommendations that serve revenue over truth.

---

### 2. Transparency

Every commercial relationship must be visible. Not in small print. Not hidden in a footer. Visible — adjacent to the content, in plain language that any reader can understand.

Transparency is not a legal obligation CULO tolerates. It is a design principle CULO enforces.

---

### 3. Honesty

AI must not invent experiences. No publisher should ever have words put in their mouth that they did not write.

CULO systems may suggest, enrich, and enhance — but the publisher's authentic voice is the final word. If a publisher has not experienced something, no CULO system will claim they have.

---

### 4. Disclosure

Every piece of content with a commercial relationship requires a visible disclosure.

There are no exceptions.

Disclosures are not optional. They are not configurable to be invisible. They cannot be turned off by publishers or businesses.

A story with an approved affiliate recommendation and no disclosure cannot be published. The Disclosure Agent blocks it.

---

### 5. Human Approval

No commercial content reaches a public surface without explicit publisher action.

AI may detect, suggest, and classify. AI may find that a publisher mentioned a brand 14 times across 6 stories. AI may identify that this brand has an active partner program. AI may calculate a confidence score.

But AI cannot publish. AI cannot approve. AI cannot accept a partnership on a publisher's behalf.

The human always approves. Always.

---

### 6. Community First

Readers come before revenue.

When CULO makes a product decision that affects the reader experience, the question is not "does this increase partner revenue?" The question is "does this make the reader's experience better?"

Readers deserve honest recommendations, clear disclosures, authentic experiences, and reliable information.

CULO will never design recommendation placements to maximise click-through at the expense of reader trust.

---

### 7. Knowledge Before Commerce

A publisher who joins CULO to share knowledge and publish stories is not an affiliate marketer. They are a person who has something to say.

Commercial relationships are available when they are natural, relevant, and genuine. They are never the reason to publish.

Publishers who use CULO only to pursue affiliate commissions will find it is not the right platform for them. The Platform Operating System is designed to reward authentic knowledge, not promotional volume.

---

### 8. Publisher Ownership

Publishers own their knowledge, their recommendations, their stories, their relationships, their reputation, and their voice.

CULO hosts and distributes — it does not own.

Publishers may leave CULO at any time and take their content with them. Data portability is a right. Recommendations approved by a publisher belong to that publisher. CULO does not retain rights to commercial relationships the publisher built.

---

### 9. Business Fairness

Businesses inside CULO earn discovery through quality — not by purchasing ranking position.

A business that spends more on a Village Pro subscription does not rank higher in publisher search results. A business that pays for a campaign does not receive inflated confidence scores.

Businesses must comply with CULO's ethical standards to participate. Any business found to be manipulating recommendations, requiring undisclosed content, or purchasing fake reviews will be removed.

---

### 10. Long-Term Relationships

CULO is not a transaction platform. It is a relationship platform.

A publisher who recommends a business once and earns a commission is worth less to the ecosystem than a publisher who recommends a business across 20 stories over 4 years because they genuinely believe in it.

CULO measures relationship depth, not transaction volume. The Trust Score rewards consistency over time, not short-term promotional activity.

---

### 11. No Manipulation

CULO will never use dark patterns to push publishers toward commercial decisions they would not otherwise make.

No artificial urgency ("this partnership closes in 24 hours"). No manufactured social proof ("312 publishers joined this program this week"). No algorithmic pressure that amplifies commercial content over authentic content.

Decisions in the Partnership Operating System must be genuine. Systems that manufacture false urgency or artificial scarcity are not permitted.

---

## Recommendation Ethics

A recommendation should only exist when:

1. The publisher genuinely knows, uses, or trusts the product, service or business.
2. The recommendation makes contextual sense within the story.
3. The recommendation improves the reader's experience.
4. The recommendation is relevant to the topic of the story.
5. The publisher has explicitly approved it.

These five conditions are cumulative. A recommendation that satisfies four but not all five should not be approved.

## Disclosure Standards

### Disclosure Types and Required Labels

| Relationship type | Disclosure label |
|---|---|
| Affiliate link (earns commission on purchase) | "Affiliate link — I may earn a commission if you purchase through this link." |
| Referral link (earns credit/reward on sign-up) | "Referral link — I may receive a benefit if you sign up through this link." |
| Sponsored content (paid to create this content) | "Sponsored — this story was created in partnership with [Business]." |
| Paid partnership (formal commercial relationship) | "Paid partnership with [Business]." |
| Gifted product (received for free) | "Gifted — I received this product at no cost. My review is my own." |
| Event sponsorship (business sponsored attendance) | "My attendance at this event was supported by [Business]." |
| Ambassador (ongoing paid or rewarded relationship) | "Brand ambassador — I have an ongoing commercial relationship with [Business]." |
| Creator program (campaign-based relationship) | "Creator program — I partnered with [Business] for this content." |
| Community partner (formal community sponsorship) | "Community partner — [Business] supports this community." |

### Disclosure placement:

Disclosures appear at the beginning of the story, before the recommended content. Not at the end. Not in a footnote.

Disclosures use plain language. No legalese. No abbreviations the average reader cannot interpret.

## AI Ethics Detailed Rules

### AI may:

- Detect entity mentions in content (businesses, brands, products, software, events, communities)
- Classify the type of mention (experience, recommendation, comparison, criticism)
- Suggest that a mention may qualify as a recommendation
- Identify businesses that have active partner programs matching the mention
- Suggest appropriate disclosure language
- Calculate confidence scores for each detection
- Update the Knowledge Graph with new entity relationships
- Surface partnership opportunities relevant to the publisher's content
- Improve metadata (topics, SEO, GEO) to improve discoverability

### AI may never:

- Invent an experience the publisher has not had
- Write a recommendation the publisher has not approved
- Pretend the publisher used a product they have not mentioned
- Insert affiliate or referral links without publisher action
- Accept a partnership on the publisher's behalf
- Approve a recommendation
- Rewrite a recommendation to change its meaning
- Remove or modify a disclosure
- Publish content with commercial relationships
- Override the publisher's rejection of a recommendation

### AI confidence and transparency:

AI confidence scores must be available internally. A detection with 40% confidence is surfaced differently from one with 90% confidence. Low-confidence detections reduce automation — they do not increase it.

Publishers must always be able to see why AI made a suggestion. "Detected because you wrote 'I use Canva every day' in paragraph 3" is meaningful. "Detected" is not.

## Publisher Rights

Publishers hold these rights and no CULO system, business or admin may override them:

**Right to reject any recommendation.** A publisher who does not want to create a commercial relationship with any business may say no. That rejection is permanent and remembered. The same recommendation may not be re-suggested.

**Right to reject any partner.** A publisher who does not want to partner with a specific business may block all future suggestions from that business.

**Right to reject monetisation entirely.** A publisher may opt out of the Partnership Operating System completely. All recommendation detection is disabled. No partnership suggestions are shown.

**Right to leave any Partner Program.** At any time, with immediate effect.

**Right to delete recommendations.** A publisher may delete any recommendation, including approved ones. The approval history is retained in the audit trail — the live recommendation is removed.

**Right to pause partnerships.** Publishers in an ongoing commercial relationship (ambassador, creator program) may pause their participation. The business is notified. Obligations during the pause period are governed by the program terms.

**Right to their own voice.** No CULO system may rewrite a recommendation in a way that changes the publisher's meaning, tone or intent.

---

## Business Rights and Limits

### Businesses may:

- Create partner programs with defined terms
- Invite publishers to campaigns
- Set commission structures and reward types
- View analytics for their own programs
- Approve or reject publisher applications
- Reward publishers fairly for recommendations and campaigns

### Businesses may never:

- Force a recommendation on a publisher
- Edit a publisher's content without their consent
- Require positive reviews or ratings as a condition of partnership
- Hide disclosures or request that disclosures be reduced or removed
- Manipulate recommendation rankings by paying CULO
- Purchase trust scores or publisher confidence ratings
- Contact publishers outside the Partnership Operating System without publisher consent

A business found violating these rules will have their Village Pro access suspended and their partner programs paused pending review.

---

## Reader Rights

Readers of content on CULO are owed:

- Honest recommendations from publishers who mean them
- Clear disclosures that are easy to understand
- Authentic experiences described in the publisher's own voice
- Independent opinions not controlled by businesses
- Transparent commercial relationships shown before the content
- Reliable information not manufactured by AI without publisher approval

CULO will never design the reading experience to deceive. A reader who trusts CULO content is the foundation of every commercial relationship in the ecosystem.

---

## Trust Score Ethics

The Trust Score is the measure of a publisher's or business's reliability in the Partnership Operating System.

### Trust is built by:

- Recommendation quality (conversion rate, reader engagement)
- Transparency (disclosure compliance record)
- Publisher consistency (stable topics, industries, content approach)
- Business behaviour (response time, fair terms, complaint history)
- Community feedback (aggregated reader sentiment, not follower count)
- Historical relationships (long-term recommendation accuracy)
- Knowledge quality (depth and accuracy of published content)

### Trust is never built by:

- Advertising spend
- Follower count
- Number of affiliate links
- Revenue generated
- Platform plan tier (Village Pro does not increase Trust Score)

A business cannot buy Trust. A publisher cannot buy Trust. Trust is earned through sustained, honest behaviour over time.

## Conflicts of Interest

### Examples of conflicts that violate this charter:

**Undisclosed affiliate links.** Publishing content with affiliate links without disclosure. This is both an ethical violation and a legal risk.

**Hidden sponsorship.** Creating sponsored content that reads as an organic recommendation without the required sponsorship disclosure.

**Misleading reviews.** Writing a positive review of a product the publisher has not used or does not genuinely recommend in exchange for payment.

**Purchased recommendations.** A business paying for placement in a publisher's story as a recommendation rather than as disclosed sponsored content.

**AI-generated fake experiences.** Using CULO's AI tools to generate "I use this product every day" type content that the publisher has not written and the experience has not been had.

**False testimonials.** Creating testimonial-style recommendations that misrepresent the publisher's actual experience.

---

### Conflict handling:

1. Community members may report suspected violations through the content report system.
2. Reports are reviewed by a Moderator within 48 hours.
3. Publishers and businesses are notified of reports and given opportunity to respond.
4. Moderator makes initial ruling. Both parties may appeal within 14 days.
5. All conflict history is retained in the audit trail.
6. Repeated violations result in suspension from the Partnership Operating System.

---

## Moderation Principles

### Community reports:

Any reader, publisher or business may report content that appears to violate these standards. Reports are anonymous. The content remains live while under review unless it is causing active harm.

### Admin review:

Moderators review reports without bias toward the business or publisher. Their only obligation is to the reader's experience and the ethical standards defined in this charter.

### Appeals:

Every moderation decision may be appealed. Appeals are reviewed by a different Moderator. Platform Owner has final authority.

### Transparency:

Moderation actions are visible to the affected publisher and business. The reason for action is always communicated in plain language.

### Audit history:

Every report, review, action and appeal is retained in the audit trail. This history informs Trust Scores over time.

## AI Safety Guardrails

| Guardrail | Rule |
|---|---|
| Commercial suggestion threshold | AI does not suggest a commercial recommendation if confidence is below 0.70 |
| Disclosure guard | If a disclosure would be removed by any action, that action is blocked |
| Approval gate | No AI output containing a commercial recommendation reaches a public surface without publisher approval |
| Voice protection | AI-suggested recommendation wording is never auto-applied — always reviewed |
| Transparency | AI confidence score is always visible to the publisher, never hidden |
| Uncertainty → less automation | Low confidence → fewer suggestions, not more automated actions |
| Human override | Any publisher can reject any AI suggestion, permanently |
| Audit | Every AI action in the Partnership Operating System is logged |

## Future Ethics Preparation

These areas require proactive ethical planning before implementation:

**Enterprise:** Enterprise clients with large publisher networks require additional safeguards against coordinated commercial activity that could compromise reader trust.

**Marketplace:** A future CULO Marketplace must apply these same standards to product listings and seller ratings.

**Plugins:** Third-party plugins that interact with the Partnership Operating System must certify compliance with this charter.

**Government and regulated industries:** Healthcare, legal, financial recommendations require additional disclosure requirements beyond this charter.

**Education:** Recommendations made in educational content require explicit disclosure of the educational context.

**International regulations:** GDPR (EU), FTC (US), ACCC (Australia), ASA (UK) rules on disclosure vary. CULO's default disclosure standards are designed to meet the most stringent requirement in any market.

**Future AI legislation:** As AI disclosure laws evolve (EU AI Act and similar), CULO's `ai_generated` field on content versions ensures the system is prepared to disclose AI involvement at any level required.

**Accessibility:** Disclosures must be accessible to readers using screen readers and assistive technologies. Disclosure placement and formatting must meet accessibility standards.

**Privacy:** Recommendation tracking must comply with privacy laws. No tracking without consent. No personal data shared with partners without explicit user consent.

**Children's content:** Any content targeting minors requires additional protections. Commercial recommendations in this context are subject to stricter rules and may require regulatory compliance beyond this charter.

## Development Rules

Every feature that touches the Partnership Operating System must answer:

1. Does this strengthen trust between publishers, businesses and readers — or potentially weaken it?
2. Is every recommendation in this feature based on a genuine publisher experience or stated knowledge?
3. Is disclosure required? If any commercial relationship exists, yes.
4. Can the publisher reject this without consequence?
5. Can the business manipulate this to hide a commercial relationship?
6. Would a reader — reading the final published output — understand the nature of the commercial relationship?
7. Would this still be ethically acceptable in five years' time, under stricter regulation?
8. Is there any path in this feature where AI makes a commercial decision without publisher approval?
9. Is there any path where a disclosure could be hidden, reduced or removed?
10. Is this feature documented in the audit trail?

If any of these questions cannot be answered with a clean yes, the feature is not ready to be built.

---

## The Final Standard

Every partnership, recommendation, campaign and commercial relationship inside CULO should pass one test:

**Would the publisher recommend this if they were not being paid?**

If yes — the Partnership Operating System enhances what was already authentic.

If no — CULO should not be the platform where that recommendation appears.
