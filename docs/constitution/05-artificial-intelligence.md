## AI Exists to Assist

Artificial intelligence is a tool.

Its role within CULO is to reduce the repetitive work that stands between a Publisher and their audience. To organise knowledge that would otherwise remain scattered. To identify connections that would otherwise be missed. To help people publish more consistently by removing the parts of the process that require effort without requiring judgment.

AI should never become the source of truth. It should never replace the human perspective at the centre of every publication. It exists to make the path between what a Publisher knows and what they are able to share as clear and as short as possible.

---

## Humans Come First

Human experience is the foundation of every publication within CULO.

The ideas should come from people. The stories should come from lived experience. The opinions should belong to the Publisher who holds them. The knowledge should be earned, not generated.

AI's role is to help Publishers express what they already know more effectively. Not to tell them what to say. Not to invent perspectives on their behalf. Not to generate knowledge from nothing and present it as if a human had arrived at it through experience.

The moment AI becomes the source of what is published, rather than the assistant to the person publishing it, the purpose of CULO is undermined. The Village exists because human knowledge has value. That value depends on the knowledge being genuinely human.

---

## Preserve the Publisher's Voice

Every Publisher has a voice.

It is shaped by their personality, their background, their way of thinking, their relationship with language, their perspective on their industry. That voice is part of what makes their knowledge worth listening to. It is what makes their work recognisably theirs.

AI should adapt to the Publisher, not the other way around.

The goal of AI assistance within CULO is amplification: to help a Publisher's voice reach further, be better organised and be more easily discovered, without changing what makes that voice distinct. A Publisher should never feel that using CULO requires them to sound like everyone else who uses CULO.

Diversity of voice strengthens the Village. Homogenisation weakens it.

---

## Reduce Friction

The legitimate purpose of AI in publishing is to remove friction.

There is work involved in publishing that requires judgment, creativity and lived experience. That work belongs to the Publisher.

There is other work — organising media, structuring information, identifying connections between related pieces of knowledge, improving readability, ensuring accessibility, suggesting how one piece of knowledge connects to another — that is repetitive and time-consuming without requiring the Publisher's unique perspective.

AI should do the second kind of work so that Publishers can focus on the first.

The measure of whether an AI capability belongs in CULO is whether it removes complexity without removing creativity. Whether it makes publishing easier without making the Publisher less present in what they publish.

---

## Never Replace Judgment

AI may make suggestions. Publishers make decisions.

Suggestions can be valuable. They can surface connections that a Publisher might have missed. They can propose structures that make knowledge more accessible. They can identify gaps or inconsistencies.

But a suggestion is always a suggestion. The Publisher reviews it, refines it, approves it or rejects it. The final judgment belongs to the person whose knowledge is being published. Their responsibility for what appears under their name remains complete.

AI should never publish automatically in situations where editorial judgment is required. Automation is appropriate for repetitive, well-defined tasks. It is never appropriate as a substitute for a Publisher's considered decision about what they are sharing with the world.

---

## Transparency

Publishers should understand how AI has contributed to their work.

If AI has suggested a structure, that suggestion should be visible. If an automation has connected two pieces of knowledge, the Publisher should be able to see it. If a caption has been generated from approved content, the origin should be clear.

AI should never operate in a deceptive manner — presenting generated content as if it were entirely the Publisher's own work, or making changes without the Publisher's awareness.

Transparency is more important than seamlessness. Publishers who understand how AI is helping them are better placed to ensure that what they publish remains authentically theirs. Trust between CULO and its Publishers depends on that clarity.

---

## Respect Human Creativity

AI should celebrate originality rather than flatten it.

One of the genuine risks of AI assistance in publishing is that it creates pressure toward a particular kind of content: the kind that AI is best at producing. Structured, competent, readable, but without the specific texture that makes a particular Publisher's perspective worth seeking out.

CULO should resist this. The goal is not to help every Publisher produce content that performs well against a common standard. The goal is to help every Publisher produce content that is unmistakably theirs.

The diversity of perspectives within the Village is one of its greatest strengths. A Publisher who thinks differently, writes in an unusual way, or approaches their subject from an unexpected angle should be supported in that distinctiveness, not nudged toward a middle ground.

---

## Learning With the Publisher

AI within CULO should improve over time by learning a Publisher's preferences, style and working methods.

The goal is to become a better assistant to each specific Publisher — one that understands how they think, what they value, and how they prefer to work — not a general-purpose tool that treats every Publisher identically.

Personalisation should serve authenticity. An AI that understands a particular Publisher well should make it easier for that Publisher to produce work that is genuinely theirs, not easier to produce work that sounds like anyone.

The relationship between AI and Publisher should improve with time and use.

---

## Ethical Use

AI within CULO should be used in a manner that respects ownership, attribution, intellectual property, privacy and consent.

AI should never misrepresent a Publisher's work or experiences. It should never present fabricated knowledge as if a Publisher had genuinely said or experienced it. It should never attribute content to a Publisher that they did not author or approve.

The ethical use of AI is not simply a compliance requirement. It is a reflection of what CULO is for. A platform built to help people preserve genuine human knowledge cannot use AI in ways that undermine the authenticity of that knowledge.

---

## The Role of AI in CULO

CULO does not exist to create artificial knowledge.

It exists to help people preserve, organise, publish and connect the knowledge they have already earned.

AI should make that process easier. It should reduce the time between a Publisher's experience and a well-organised, accessible, connected publication. It should help knowledge reach the people who need it more effectively.

It should never make publishing less human.

The standard by which every AI capability in CULO should be evaluated is this: does this help the Publisher share their genuine knowledge more effectively, or does it replace the Publisher's genuine knowledge with something that only resembles it?

The first is valuable. The second is contrary to everything CULO exists to do.
