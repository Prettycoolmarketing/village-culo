## Who Is a Publisher?

A Publisher is anyone who shares genuine knowledge, experience, expertise or stories with the intention of helping others.

Publishing is not a profession reserved for journalists, authors or media companies. It is something any person can do, and something every person with earned knowledge has reason to do.

A founder who has built and grown a business is a Publisher. A tradesperson who has spent decades mastering their craft is a Publisher. A teacher who has developed a method that changes how students understand something difficult is a Publisher. A parent who has navigated something hard and wants to help others do the same is a Publisher. A coach, an artist, a nurse, a chef, a mechanic — any person who has learned something through doing it and is willing to share it honestly is a Publisher.

The only requirement for being a Publisher within CULO is the genuine intention to help.

## What Does a Publisher Create?

Publishers create knowledge. The format that knowledge takes depends on what best expresses it.

Some knowledge is best expressed as a written story. Some is better demonstrated through video. Some should live as a frequently asked question, answered with care. Some should be taught through a structured course. Some belongs in a book that someone can return to over time.

The formats available to a Publisher include:

Blogs. Reels. Carousels. Voice overs. Vlogs. Talking head videos. Books. Podcasts. Workshops. Case studies. FAQs. Resources. Products. Services. Talks.

Each format is a different expression of the same underlying knowledge. A Publisher does not need to use every format. They need to choose the formats that best serve what they know and who they are trying to help.

Every format a Publisher creates contributes to one connected body of work. The pieces are not separate. They build on one another, reinforce one another, and together create a picture of the Publisher's expertise and experience that no single piece could capture alone.

---

## Publishing Is an Asset

Every piece of content a Publisher creates should be treated as a long-term asset, not a temporary post.

A social media post is designed to create a moment. A publication is designed to create a resource. The distinction matters because it changes how the work is approached.

A publication should be written clearly, structured well, and connected to the broader body of the Publisher's work. It should be as useful to someone who finds it in three years as it is to someone who finds it today. It should earn its place in the Village through the value it provides, not through the timing of its release.

Publishing should increase discoverability over time. Each new publication should make it easier for people to find the Publisher's previous work. Each new connection should strengthen the ecosystem of knowledge that already exists.

Knowledge compounds. A Publisher who builds consistently over years creates something significantly more valuable than the sum of its individual parts.

---

## Authenticity

CULO exists to amplify a Publisher's real voice, not to replace it.

Every Publisher has a personality, a perspective and a way of communicating that belongs to them. That voice is what makes their knowledge worth listening to. It is what differentiates their work from anyone else who might know similar things.

AI should help a Publisher organise, structure and distribute their knowledge more effectively. It should surface connections, reduce repetitive work and improve discoverability. It should never flatten the Publisher's voice into something generic.

The stories should come from the Publisher's life. The ideas should come from the Publisher's thinking. The opinions should belong to the Publisher.

Publishers remain entirely responsible for the truth and authenticity of what they publish. CULO provides tools to help them publish well. It does not take responsibility for the knowledge itself.

---

## Ownership

Publishers own their knowledge.

They own their stories, their ideas, their expertise and the content they create. They choose what to publish, when to update it and whether to archive or remove it.

CULO provides the infrastructure for publishing. It does not claim ownership of what is published within it.

This principle is not negotiable. Knowledge earned through lived experience belongs to the person who earned it. A platform that helps someone publish their knowledge does not acquire rights to that knowledge simply by hosting it.

Publishers should always be able to take their knowledge with them.

---

## Relationships

Publishing within CULO does not create isolated pages. It creates connections.

Every Publisher becomes connected to the businesses they have built, the stories they have told, the ideas they have developed, the expertise they have demonstrated, the questions they have answered, the services they offer, the products they have created, the talks they have given, and the communities they belong to.

These connections form a knowledge graph: a living map of everything a Publisher knows and has contributed. That map makes the Publisher more discoverable. It makes each individual piece of knowledge more meaningful. It makes the relationship between different areas of a Publisher's work visible to anyone exploring the Village.

Publishing creates relationships. Relationships create understanding. Understanding creates trust. Trust creates opportunity.

---

## Responsibility

Publishing carries responsibility.

Publishers should publish honestly. They should represent their experience and expertise accurately. They should acknowledge when they do not know something. They should update their knowledge when circumstances change and what they previously published is no longer accurate.

Publishers should credit the people who contributed to their work. Collaboration should be acknowledged. Intellectual property should be respected.

Publishers should approach the Village as a place they are contributing to, not simply benefiting from. Every meaningful publication strengthens the Village. Every misleading or careless publication weakens it.

The strength of the Village depends on the integrity of its Publishers. That integrity is ultimately the responsibility of each person who publishes within it.
