## Revenue Supports the Mission

CULO exists to help people preserve, publish and grow their knowledge.

Sustaining that mission requires revenue. Without it, the infrastructure cannot be maintained, the Village cannot grow and the work of improving the platform cannot continue. Revenue is not a distraction from the mission — it is what makes the mission sustainable.

The purpose of monetisation in CULO is to fund and strengthen the publishing ecosystem, not to maximise extraction from it. Profit matters because it enables longevity. It is never the reason CULO exists.

---

## Publishers Should Benefit

Wherever possible, Publishers should have genuine opportunities to earn from the knowledge they create.

A Publisher who contributes meaningfully to the Village — who builds a body of work, develops a reputation for expertise, and consistently helps the people who find them — should be supported in turning that contribution into sustainable income.

Publishing should create real economic opportunity: for services offered, products sold, workshops attended, books read, talks booked, collaborations formed. CULO should help make those opportunities visible and accessible.

The goal is to help Publishers build sustainable businesses around their genuine expertise, not to create dependency on a platform.

---

## Fair Exchange

Every paid feature within CULO should provide clear and honest value.

Publishers should understand exactly what they receive in return for any investment they make. The value should be real, not manufactured by artificial limitations. Pricing should reflect usefulness, not the exploitation of a Publisher's need.

CULO should never create unnecessary friction or withhold basic functionality in order to drive upgrades. Every decision about what is included in a paid tier should be made on the basis of what is fair, not what is most effective at extracting revenue.

Trust should always outweigh short-term revenue.

---

## Transparent Partnerships

CULO may enter into relationships with software companies, brands, publishers, marketplaces and other organisations.

Every such relationship should exist because it genuinely helps Publishers. Partnerships that benefit CULO commercially but provide little or no value to the people who use the platform are not aligned with its purpose.

When a commercial relationship exists, it should be transparent. Sponsored content should be clearly identified. Affiliated recommendations should be disclosed. Publishers should never encounter a recommendation within the Village that they cannot trust because they cannot tell whether it is commercially motivated.

Authenticity is what makes recommendations within the Village valuable. Transparency is what protects that authenticity.

---

## Referral and Partner Programs

CULO should create opportunities for Publishers to earn through genuine recommendations.

Publishers who recommend products, services or resources they genuinely use and believe in should be able to benefit from doing so. Referral programs should reward honest advocacy, not manipulation.

Any affiliate or partner program should be clearly disclosed. Publishers should retain full control over how recommendations appear within their work. Earning from a recommendation should never require a Publisher to say something they do not believe.

Trust is the currency that makes referrals valuable. Programs that compromise that trust compromise the value they were designed to create.

---

## Marketplace Philosophy

The Village should help people discover books, templates, courses, services, workshops, resources, digital products and physical products because those things help people — not because they generate the highest commercial return.

The standard for visibility in any marketplace within CULO should be quality and genuine usefulness. Commercial incentives should never override those standards.

A product that is excellent should be more discoverable than a product that pays the highest commission. The moment commercial incentives begin to distort what people find in the Village, the Village becomes less trustworthy. And a Village that cannot be trusted has no value to anyone.

---

## Monetisation Should Never Reduce Trust

Trust is CULO's most valuable asset.

People use the Village because they trust that what they find there is honest, well-curated and genuinely useful. Publishers share their knowledge in the Village because they trust that it will be treated with integrity. That trust is the foundation everything else depends on.

No revenue decision should compromise it. Advertising that distorts content, partnerships that shape recommendations for commercial reasons, pricing models that exploit Publishers' needs, marketplace structures that privilege commercial relationships over quality — all of these reduce trust, and any reduction in trust reduces the value of the Village.

> **Revenue should reward contribution, not exploit attention.**

---

## Reward Contribution

The Publishers who contribute most meaningfully to the Village should benefit most from being part of it.

CULO should create visibility, opportunity, and where possible, income for the people whose knowledge and effort strengthen the ecosystem. Recognition within the Village should be tied to genuine contribution rather than to commercial investment or follower counts.

The goal is for the Village to become a place where building honestly, sharing generously and contributing consistently creates real, tangible benefit — not just goodwill.

---

## Sustainable Growth

Growth should be measured by the value created for Publishers and communities, not by user numbers or revenue alone.

Revenue should be reinvested into improving the publishing ecosystem. Every investment in the platform should make it easier for Publishers to share their knowledge, more valuable for people who discover it, and more sustainable for the community that depends on it.

When Publishers succeed, CULO succeeds. When CULO improves, Publishers succeed. That relationship should remain at the centre of every decision about how revenue is generated and used.

---

## The Purpose of Monetisation

Monetisation is not the purpose of CULO.

It is the engine that allows the mission to continue.

Revenue should make it easier for people to publish, preserve and discover knowledge. It should fund the infrastructure that makes genuine, permanent, connected publishing possible. It should create opportunities for Publishers to build lasting work and sustainable businesses.

When Publishers succeed, the Village succeeds. When the Village succeeds, CULO succeeds. That alignment is not incidental. It is the only model of monetisation that remains consistent with what CULO exists to do.
